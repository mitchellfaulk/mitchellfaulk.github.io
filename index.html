About

Hello! And welcome to my personal page.

I'm trained as a mathematician, with a PhD from Columbia University and a BS from the University of Notre Dame. After my studies, I completed a postdoctoral fellowship at Vanderbilt University, where I continued to research problems in geometry that are motivated, in part, by equations due to Einstein describing the shape of the universe.

In that time, I've also taught and mentored hundreds of students on a variety of topics in mathematics, including, but not limited to, real analysis, convex optimization, linear algebra, differential equations, and multivariable Calculus.

Recently I've been teaching myself computer sciece and data science, and I've shared some updates on my personal blog. You can also find some of my recent projects displayed below.

Recent Projects

SET game simulator +

A collection of python scripts to simulate the card game SET in order to study statistics from the game (and refute some statistics listed in the game's instruction manual).

The scripts also permit simulations of other analogous 'games' that could be played in other dimensions.
