Publications

  • Asymptotically conical Calabi-Yau orbifolds, I

    [abstract] [pdf] arXiv preprint (2018)

    A notion of asymptotically conical Kahler orbifold is introduced, and, following previous existence results in the setting of asymptotically conical manifolds, it is shown that a certain complex Monge-Ampere equation admits a rapidly decaying solution (which is unique for certain intervals of decay rates), allowing one to construct Kahler metrics with prescribed Ricci forms. In particular, if the orbifold has trivial canonical bundle, then Ricci-flat metrics can be constructed, provided certain additional hypotheses are met. This implies for example that orbifold crepant partial resolutions of varieties associated to Calabi-Yau cones admit a one-parameter family of Calabi-Yau metrics in each Kahler class that contains positive (1,1)-forms.

  • Yau's theorem for effective orbiflds

    [abstract] [pdf] Expositiones Mathematicae doi: 10.1016/j.exmath.2018.07.003 (2018)

    In 1978 Yau (Yau, 1978) confirmed a conjecture due to Calabi (1954) stating the existence of Kahler metrics with prescribed Ricci forms on compact Kahler manifolds. A version of this statement for effective orbifolds can be found in the literature (Joyce, 2000; Boyer and Galicki, 2008; Demailly and Kollar, 2001). In this expository article, we provide details for a proof of this orbifold version of the statement by adapting Yau's original continuity method to the setting of effective orbifolds in order to solve a Monge–Ampere equation. We then outline how to obtain Kahler–Einstein metrics on orbifolds with negative first Chern class by solving a slightly different Monge–Ampere equation. We conclude by listing some explicit examples of Calabi–Yau orbifolds, which consequently admit Ricci flat metrics by Yau's theorem for effective orbifolds.

  • (with M. Farber, C. Johnson, and E. Marzion) Exact results for perturbation to total positivity and to total nonnegativity

    [abstract] [pdf] Electron. J. Linear Algebra 27 (2014)

    A study of the maximum number of equal entries in totally positive and totally nonsingular m-by-n, matrices for small values of m and n, is presented. Equal entries correspond to entries of the totally nonnegative matrix J that are not changed in producing a TP or TNS matrix. It is shown that the maximum number of equal entries in a 7-by-7 totally positive matrix is strictly smaller than that for a 7-by-7 totally non-singular matrix, but, this is the first pair (m, n) for which these maximum numbers differ. Using point-line geometry in the projective plane, a family of values for (m,n) for which these maximum numbers differ is presented. Generalization to the Hadamard core, as well as larger projective planes is also established. Finally, the relationship with C4 free graphs, along with a method for producing symmetric T P matrices with maximal symmetric arrangements of equal entries is discussed.

  • (with M. Farber, C. Johnson, and E. Marzion) Equal entries in totally positive matrices

    [abstract] [pdf] Linear Algebra Appl. 454 (2014), 91--106

    We show that the maximal number of equal entries in a totally positive (resp. totally nonsingular) n-by-n matrix is \Theta(n4/3) (resp. \Theta(n3/2)). Relationships with point-line incidences in the plane, Bruhat order of permutations, and TP completability are also presented. We also examine the number and positionings of equal 2-by-2 minors in a 2-by-nTP matrix, and give a relationship between the location of equal 2-by-2 minors and outerplanar graphs.
